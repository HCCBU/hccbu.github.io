Between 15th of February and the 8th of March, we delivered, along with [Dr Peter Butcher](https://www.bangor.ac.uk/staff/computer-science-electronic-engineering/peter-butcher-080986/en), a course on XReality, to [Airbus](https://www.airbus.com/en) employees. The course is part of the [DSP Centre's](https://dsp-centre.bangor.ac.uk/) Educational College and was delivered within the [Skills Factory programme](https://www.bangor.ac.uk/news/bangor-university-and-coleg-cambria-announce-a-new-collaborative-relationship), for which the [School of Computer Science and Electronic Engineering](https://www.bangor.ac.uk/computer-science-and-electronic-engineering/index.php.en), [Bangor University](https://www.bangor.ac.uk/) is a primary contributor. 


<img src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/XR_Airbus.jpg" alt="XReality course delivered at Airbus" style="margin: 10 auto; width: 800px;">

The course provided and introduction to Virtual, Augmented and Mixed Reality, discussed some of the challenges of these paradigms, and presented some of our demos in [Immersive Analytics]({{ site.baseurl }}/projects/ia/) (see image above, of the VRIA framework in MR, through the Microsoft HoloLens 2). The course was delivered over four sessions.
